# GeoNeo AI — Positioning

**This is the single source of truth for who we serve and what we promise. Every
product decision, feature choice, and piece of copy should reference this doc.
If something in code or marketing contradicts it, this doc wins.**

---

## One-sentence pitch

> GeoNeo AI shows home-service contractors in the Ozarks exactly why they're
> losing local Google and ChatGPT searches to competitors — and gives them the
> specific fixes to make this week, not a 40-page report.

## Who we serve

**Primary (Year 1):** Home-service contractor businesses in the Ozarks region
(Southwest Missouri + Northwest Arkansas). Owner-operator or small team (1-15
employees). No in-house marketing staff.

**Target verticals (8):**

1. Plumbing
2. HVAC / heating & cooling
3. Roofing
4. Electrical
5. Pest control
6. Tree service
7. Garage door service
8. Water / fire / mold restoration

**Target geography (Year 1):** Springfield MO, Branson MO, Joplin MO, Ozark MO,
Nixa MO, Republic MO, Fayetteville AR, Rogers AR, Bentonville AR, Little Rock AR.

**Expansion (Year 2):** Remainder of MO, AR, OK, KS. Same verticals.

**We are not for:**
- Enterprise brands or franchises (they have agencies)
- E-commerce or SaaS companies (different playbook)
- Agencies buying in bulk (different tool)
- Consumers (this is B2B, small-business)

## The problem we solve

Home-service contractors spend $300-$2,000/month on marketing and can't tell if
it's working. They're stuck between three bad options:

1. **SEO agencies** — $800-$3,000/mo, opaque monthly reports, no accountability.
2. **Lead-gen platforms** (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack) — inflated per-lead
   prices, shared leads, race-to-the-bottom.
3. **DIY tools** (Semrush, Moz, BrightLocal) — built for marketers, not for a
   plumber with 20 minutes between jobs.

Meanwhile, a structural shift is happening that none of those options is
addressing: customers are asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and
Gemini for local recommendations. Most contractors don't even know this is
happening, and no one is telling them whether they're getting recommended.

## The promise

Every week, in plain English, we tell each customer:

1. **What local searches and AI searches you appeared in this week**
   (e.g. "ChatGPT recommended Able Plumbing 7 times; recommended you 0 times").
2. **Who beat you, and why** (specific competitors, specific reasons).
3. **Three specific changes to make this week** to fix it — changes the owner
   can do in an hour, or hand to any web person to implement.

No dashboards the owner won't open. No jargon. No annual contracts.

## Why we can win

1. **We watch AI search, not just Google.** Most local-SEO tools ignore
   ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews. This is the emerging
   battleground and we lead with it.
2. **We translate.** Our output is sentences a contractor acts on, not
   dashboards a marketer interprets.
3. **We're local.** Branson-based. We can drive to every Year-1 customer.
   Face-to-face closes at ~10× cold email.
4. **No retainer.** Flat monthly price. Cancel anytime. This alone breaks the
   agency objection.

## Success indicators (what "working" means)

- **Month 1:** First 5 paying Starter customers in SW Missouri.
- **Month 3:** 20 paying customers, $3K MRR, one public case study per vertical.
- **Month 6:** 50 paying customers, $8K MRR, NW Arkansas opened.
- **Month 12:** 100 paying customers, $15-20K MRR, first Growth-tier upsells
  converting from Starter.

## Non-negotiables

- Output must be owner-readable, not marketer-readable.
- Every audit must answer "what do I do this week?" with a concrete list.
- AI-search visibility is always in the report, not an upsell bolt-on.
- We never hide a finding behind a paywall — we show *what's wrong* for free
  and charge for *the prioritized implementation path*.
- We never lock into contracts. Month-to-month, cancel anytime.

## What this positioning explicitly rejects

- "Platform for agencies" — we'd lose focus.
- "Tool for every industry" — dilutes the product and the marketing.
- "AI-only" as a narrow positioning — we still need Google/Maps parity;
  contractors care about phones ringing, not which engine recommended them.
- Big-city first — we'd be outspent. Ozarks first, then expand.
